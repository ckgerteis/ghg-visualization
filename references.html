Reference List for GHG Visualization Data

All data sources and scientific references used in the greenhouse gas visualization

Atmospheric Concentration Data (CO₂, CH₄, N₂O)

Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)

  • Pre-industrial to present concentrations: NOAA Global Monitoring Laboratory. "Trends in Atmospheric Carbon Dioxide." https://gml.noaa.gov/ccgg/trends/
  • Historical ice core data: Law Dome ice cores via NOAA National Centers for Environmental Information; Etheridge, D.M., et al. (1996). "Natural and anthropogenic changes in atmospheric CO₂ over the last 1000 years from air in Antarctic ice and firn." Journal of Geophysical Research, 101, 4115-4128.
  • Pre-industrial baseline (280 ppm): IPCC (2013). Climate Change 2013: The Physical Science Basis, Chapter 8.3.2.

Methane (CH₄)

  • Current and historical concentrations: NOAA Global Monitoring Laboratory. "Trends in Atmospheric Methane." https://gml.noaa.gov/ccgg/trends_ch4/
  • Pre-industrial value (722 ppb): Etheridge, D.M., L.P. Steele, R.J. Francey, and R.L. Langenfelds. (1998). "Atmospheric methane between 1000 A.D. and present: evidence of anthropogenic emissions and climatic variability." Journal of Geophysical Research, 103, 15979-15996.
  • Historical trends and annual growth rates: Lan, X., K.W. Thoning, and E.J. Dlugokencky. "Trends in globally-averaged CH₄, N₂O, and SF₆ determined from NOAA Global Monitoring Laboratory measurements." https://doi.org/10.15138/P8XG-AA10

Nitrous Oxide (N₂O)

  • Current and historical concentrations: NOAA Global Monitoring Laboratory. "Trends in Atmospheric Nitrous Oxide." https://gml.noaa.gov/ccgg/trends_n2o/
  • Pre-industrial value (270 ppb): MacFarling Meure, C., et al. (2006). "The Law Dome CO₂, CH₄ and N₂O Ice Core Records Extended to 2000 years BP." Geophysical Research Letters, 33, L14810.
  • Global N₂O Budget: Tian, H., et al. (2024). "Global nitrous oxide budget (1980–2020)." Earth System Science Data, 16, 2543-2604. https://essd.copernicus.org/articles/16/2543/2024/

Global Warming Potential (GWP) Values

  • GWP-20 and GWP-100 values: IPCC (2021). Climate Change 2021: The Physical Science Basis. Contribution of Working Group I to the Sixth Assessment Report (AR6), Table 7.15 and Chapter 7.
  • Reference compilation: GHG Protocol. "Global Warming Potential Values" (August 2024). https://ghgprotocol.org/
  • EPA guidance: U.S. Environmental Protection Agency. "Understanding Global Warming Potentials." https://www.epa.gov/ghgemissions/understanding-global-warming-potentials

Radiative Forcing Data

  • Radiative forcing values (W/m²): NOAA Global Monitoring Laboratory. "Annual Greenhouse Gas Index (AGGI)." https://gml.noaa.gov/aggi/aggi.html
  • Forcing calculations methodology: Forster, P., et al. (2021). "The Earth's Energy Budget, Climate Feedbacks, and Climate Sensitivity." In Climate Change 2021: The Physical Science Basis, IPCC AR6 WGI, Chapter 7.

Methane Emission Sources

  • Source breakdown percentages: Climate and Clean Air Coalition (CCAC) and United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP). (2021). Global Methane Assessment: Benefits and Costs of Mitigating Methane Emissions.
  • Sectoral emissions: Global Carbon Project. (2020). "Global Methane Budget 2000–2017." Saunois, M., et al. (2020). "The Global Methane Budget 2000–2017." Earth System Science Data, 12, 1561-1623.
  • U.S. emissions breakdown: U.S. Environmental Protection Agency. "Methane Emissions." https://www.epa.gov/ghgemissions/methane-emissions

Nitrous Oxide Emission Sources

  • Source breakdown and agricultural contribution (~74%): Tian, H., et al. (2020). "A comprehensive quantification of global nitrous oxide sources and sinks." Nature, 586, 248-256.
  • Agricultural soil emissions: U.S. Environmental Protection Agency. "Nitrous Oxide Emissions." https://www.epa.gov/ghgemissions/nitrous-oxide-emissions
  • Fertilizer contribution (~57% of agricultural soils): Global Carbon Project Global N₂O Budget (2024); CSIRO. "Food has a climate problem: Nitrous oxide emissions are accelerating."

Agricultural Data (Fertilizer and Livestock)

Synthetic Nitrogen Fertilizer Use

  • Historical fertilizer data (1961–2020): Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO). FAOSTAT: Land, Inputs and Sustainability: Fertilizers by Nutrient. https://www.fao.org/faostat/
  • Crop-specific nitrogen fertilization: Lu, C., et al. (2023). "Global crop-specific nitrogen fertilization dataset in 1961–2020." Scientific Data, 10, 617.
  • Global fertilizer trends: International Fertilizer Association (IFA). IFASTAT Database.

Global Livestock Numbers

  • Historical livestock populations (1961–present): Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO). FAOSTAT: Production: Livestock Primary. https://www.fao.org/faostat/
  • Livestock trends analysis: Thornton, P.K. (2010). "Livestock production: recent trends, future prospects." Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B, 365, 2853-2867.

General References

  • IPCC AR6 Summary: Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (2021). Climate Change 2021: The Physical Science Basis. Cambridge University Press.
  • Our World in Data: Ritchie, H., Rosado, P., and Roser, M. "CO₂ and Greenhouse Gas Emissions" and "Meat and Dairy Production." https://ourworldindata.org/
  • Atmospheric concentration tracking: 2 Degrees Institute. CO₂ Levels (https://www.co2levels.org/), Methane Levels (https://www.methanelevels.org/), N₂O Levels (https://www.n2olevels.org/)

Notes on Data Accuracy

Some specific values in the visualization appear to be rounded or interpolated from the primary sources. The percentage breakdowns for methane and N₂O sources vary slightly across studies depending on methodology and reference year. The visualization's figures align with the ranges reported in the UNEP Global Methane Assessment (2021) and the Global Carbon Project's Global Nitrous Oxide Budget (2024).
